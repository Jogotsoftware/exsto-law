**Re:** Formation of **Pine Hollow Roasters, LLC** (North Carolina LLC) and preparation of operating agreement

Dear Marcus and Priya,

Thank you for engaging Pacheco Law Firm, PLLC (the "**Firm**") to assist with the formation of your new North Carolina limited liability company. This letter confirms the terms of our engagement (the "**Engagement**"). When you sign below and return a copy, this letter becomes the written agreement between you and the Firm for the work described.

## 1. Scope of Representation

The Firm will represent both of you, jointly, in connection with the following matters (the "**Matter**"):

- Preparation and filing of Articles of Organization for **Pine Hollow Roasters, LLC** (the "**Company**") with the North Carolina Secretary of State.
- Preparation of an Operating Agreement for the Company, based on the intake questionnaire each of you submitted and the consultation we held on May 22, 2026.
- Preparation of an intellectual property assignment from Marcus Holloway to the Company covering the coffee roasting formulations, blend recipes, and single-origin roast profiles identified during the consultation.
- Preparation of a spousal consent for Marcus's spouse, Sarah Holloway.
- Service as the Company's registered agent in North Carolina for a period of twelve (12) months from the Effective Date of the Articles of Organization, included at no additional charge.
- One round of revisions to the Operating Agreement and the related exhibits after you have reviewed the first draft.

The following matters are **outside the scope of this Engagement** unless we mutually agree in writing to expand it:

- Trademark search, application, or registration for the "Pine Hollow Roasters" name or any associated logo or trade dress.
- Securities-law advice, including the application of federal or North Carolina securities laws to any future capital raise from outside investors.
- Tax planning beyond default LLC partnership treatment, including any later election to be taxed as an S corporation.
- Employment agreements, independent-contractor agreements, or HR-related advice.
- Commercial lease review or negotiation, including the lease for the 213 Riverside Drive space.
- Litigation, dispute resolution, or any matter not expressly listed above.

If at any time during the Engagement you ask the Firm to undertake any of the foregoing or any other matter not within the original scope, the Firm will provide a written supplemental engagement letter for that work before commencing it.

## 2. Joint Representation

You have asked the Firm to represent both of you jointly in this Matter. Joint representation is appropriate when the clients' interests are aligned, and at this stage of forming a new business venture together, your interests are aligned. You should be aware, however, that joint representation has the following implications:

- The Firm cannot keep confidences between you. Anything either of you tells the Firm in connection with the Matter is available to the other.
- If a material conflict of interest arises between you during the course of the Engagement, the Firm will be required to withdraw from representing both of you, and each of you will need to retain separate counsel.
- The Firm cannot favor one of you over the other in negotiation or advice.

By signing below, each of you acknowledges these implications and consents to joint representation.

## 3. Fees and Costs

The Firm's fee for the Matter is **three thousand eight hundred United States dollars (US$3,800)**, a flat fee. The flat fee covers the work described in Section 1, including the one round of revisions and the first twelve months of registered-agent service.

The flat fee does not include third-party costs, which will be billed to you at the Firm's cost:

- North Carolina Secretary of State filing fee for the Articles of Organization (currently US$125, payable upon filing).
- Any expedited-processing fee, if you request the Firm to file expedited (currently US$200 in addition to the standard filing fee).
- Any other governmental filing fees or third-party charges that you specifically authorize.

The flat fee is due as follows: 50% (US$1,900) upon execution of this letter; 50% (US$1,900) upon the filing of the Articles of Organization. Filing fees are due in full at the time of filing.

If you ask the Firm to undertake work outside the scope of this Engagement, that work will be billed separately at the Firm's then-current hourly rates (currently US$285/hour for attorney time), with a written supplemental engagement letter as described in Section 1.

## 4. Client Responsibilities

You agree to:

- Provide accurate and complete information needed for the Matter, including the items identified in the consultation as needing follow-up (the written appraisal of the Probat UG-22 roaster, the inventory of coffee roasting formulations and recipes for Exhibit B-1, and Sarah Holloway's signed spousal consent).
- Respond promptly to requests for information, signatures, or decisions. Delays on your end may delay the filing of the Articles of Organization.
- Notify the Firm promptly of any changes that may affect the Matter, including changes to the planned ownership percentages, capital contributions, principal office, or management structure.
- Review draft documents carefully and identify any errors, omissions, or disagreements before signing.

## 5. Communications and Records

The Firm will communicate with you primarily by email at the addresses on file. By signing below, you consent to receive communications related to the Matter electronically. You may revoke this consent at any time by written notice to the Firm; thereafter the Firm will use ordinary first-class mail.

The Firm will retain a copy of the executed Operating Agreement, the Articles of Organization, and the related exhibits in the Firm's client files for the period required by applicable rules and the Firm's records-retention policy.

## 6. Conflicts and Confidentiality

The Firm has performed a conflicts check and is not aware of any conflict that would prevent representation in this Matter, subject to the joint-representation disclosure in Section 2. All communications between you and the Firm in connection with the Matter will be treated as confidential and protected by the attorney-client privilege, subject to applicable exceptions under North Carolina law and the joint-representation implications described in Section 2.

## 7. Termination

Either of you, or the Firm, may terminate this Engagement at any time upon written notice to the others. Upon termination, you will be responsible for fees and costs incurred through the termination date. If the Engagement is terminated after the Articles of Organization have been filed but before the Operating Agreement is signed, the Firm will deliver the latest draft of the Operating Agreement to you and your work product will be yours to take to substitute counsel.

## 8. Disputes Regarding This Engagement

Any dispute about this Engagement, the fees, or the work performed by the Firm shall be resolved first by good-faith negotiation between you and the Firm. If negotiation does not produce a resolution within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to the North Carolina State Bar's Fee Dispute Resolution Program if the dispute concerns fees, or to non-binding mediation through the Buncombe County Mediation Center if the dispute concerns other matters. This Section 8 does not waive any rights you or the Firm would otherwise have under the North Carolina Rules of Professional Conduct.

## 9. Governing Law

This Engagement and any dispute arising from it are governed by the laws of the State of North Carolina.

## Ambiguities flagged by drafting agent

1. **Registered-agent service inclusion.** The intake noted preparation and filing of Articles + OA + engagement letter and one round of revisions, but did not address whether the Firm's registered-agent service for the first twelve months is bundled into the flat fee. The consultation implied the Firm would act as registered agent; the draft bundles twelve months at no additional charge as a courtesy. Confirm whether twelve months is the right inclusion period; one year is the standard.
2. **Hourly rate.** Section 3 references the Firm's hourly rate of US$285/hour for work outside the scope. Confirm the current rate; this draft uses the rate disclosed during the consultation but Juan Carlos may want to verify before sending.
3. **Joint-representation conflict procedure.** Section 2 describes what happens if a material conflict arises between Marcus and Priya during the Engagement. Standard practice is to also have a separate written acknowledgment of joint representation signed by each Member. Whether to include that as a separate document or rely on this engagement letter is an attorney judgment call.
4. **Fee payment timing.** Section 3 sets the 50/50 payment schedule. The intake did not address payment timing. Confirm with Marcus and Priya before sending.
