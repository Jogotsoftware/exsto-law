# Operating Agreement of Pine Hollow Roasters, LLC

**State of Formation:** North Carolina
**Effective Date:** June 15, 2026

This Operating Agreement (this "**Agreement**") of **Pine Hollow Roasters, LLC** (the "**Company**"), a North Carolina limited liability company, is entered into and shall be effective as of the Effective Date set forth above, by and among **Marcus Holloway** and **Priya Iyer**, who together constitute all of the members of the Company (each, a "**Member**" and collectively, the "**Members**").

## Article I — Formation

1.1 **Formation.** The Company was formed under and pursuant to the North Carolina Limited Liability Company Act, N.C. Gen. Stat. Chapter 57D (the "**Act**"), upon the filing of its Articles of Organization with the North Carolina Secretary of State.

1.2 **Name.** The name of the Company is **Pine Hollow Roasters, LLC**.

1.3 **Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be located at **213 Riverside Drive, Asheville, North Carolina 28801**, or such other location within the State of North Carolina as the Manager may designate.

1.4 **Registered Agent and Registered Office.** The Company's registered agent in the State of North Carolina is **Pacheco Law Firm, PLLC**, located at **418 Patton Avenue, Suite 210, Asheville, North Carolina 28801**. The Members may change the registered agent or registered office upon notice to the North Carolina Secretary of State.

1.5 **Purpose.** The purpose of the Company is to engage in the specialty coffee roasting, retail sale of coffee and prepared beverages, and wholesale distribution of roasted coffee to restaurants and cafés in North Carolina and adjoining states, and to engage in any other lawful act or activity for which a limited liability company may be organized under the Act.

1.6 **Term.** The Company shall have perpetual existence unless dissolved earlier in accordance with Article VI of this Agreement or the Act.

1.7 **Tax Classification.** The Company shall be classified as a partnership for federal income tax purposes for so long as it has more than one Member, and the Members shall not file an election under Treasury Regulations Section 301.7701-3 to be treated as an association taxable as a corporation, *except* by the affirmative written consent of Members holding a majority of the membership interests. The Members acknowledge that they intend to revisit the question of an S-corporation election at or near the close of the Company's first full fiscal year.

## Article II — Members, Capital Contributions, and Capital Accounts

2.1 **Members.** The names, addresses, capital contributions, and ownership percentages (each, a "**Percentage Interest**") of the Members are set forth on **Schedule A** attached to this Agreement and incorporated by reference.

2.2 **Initial Capital Contributions.** Each Member has made, or has agreed to make on or before the Effective Date, the initial capital contribution set forth opposite such Member's name on Schedule A. Marcus Holloway's contribution consists of (a) eighty-five thousand United States dollars (US$85,000) in cash and (b) one Probat UG-22 thirty-kilogram drum coffee roaster, refurbished, valued at forty-two thousand United States dollars (US$42,000) based on a written appraisal dated March 14, 2026, a copy of which is attached as **Exhibit A**. Priya Iyer's contribution consists of one hundred three thousand United States dollars (US$103,000) in cash.

2.3 **No Mandatory Additional Capital Contributions.** No Member shall be obligated to contribute additional capital to the Company. No Member shall be subject to dilution of such Member's Percentage Interest by reason of a failure to contribute additional capital.

2.4 **Member Loans.** If at any time the Manager reasonably determines that the Company requires funds in excess of its then-available cash and working capital reserve, either Member may, but is not required to, advance the required amount to the Company as a loan (a "**Member Loan**"). Each Member Loan shall be evidenced by a written promissory note, shall bear interest at a rate equal to the prime rate published by *The Wall Street Journal* on the date of the loan plus two percent (2.00%) per annum, and shall be repayable in full, including all accrued interest, before any further distributions are made to the Members under Article IV. If neither Member is willing or able to make a Member Loan within thirty (30) days after the Manager's written request and the Company nevertheless requires additional funds to continue operations, the Members shall convene and determine by majority vote whether to seek third-party financing, admit a new Member subject to Section 5.5, or commence the winding-up of the Company under Article VI.

2.5 **Capital Accounts.** A separate capital account shall be maintained for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv). No Member shall be entitled to interest on such Member's capital contribution or capital account. No Member shall have the right to withdraw any part of such Member's capital contribution except upon dissolution and winding up of the Company.

2.6 **Section 704(c) Allocations.** Solely for federal income tax purposes, items of income, gain, loss, and deduction with respect to the Probat UG-22 roaster contributed by Marcus Holloway shall be allocated among the Members so as to take account of the variation between the fair market value of the asset as determined under Section 2.2 (US$42,000) and its adjusted tax basis at the time of contribution, all in accordance with Section 704(c) of the Internal Revenue Code and the regulations thereunder, using the traditional method described in Treasury Regulations Section 1.704-3(b).

## Article III — Management

3.1 **Manager-Managed.** The Company is **manager-managed** within the meaning of N.C. Gen. Stat. § 57D-3-20. Marcus Holloway shall serve as the initial Manager and shall continue in such capacity until such Manager's resignation, death, disability, removal by Members holding a majority of the Percentage Interests not held by such Manager, or the dissolution of the Company.

3.2 **Authority of the Manager.** Subject to Sections 3.3 and 3.4, the Manager has full and exclusive authority to manage the business and affairs of the Company, including without limitation: (a) entering into contracts and incurring obligations in the ordinary course; (b) hiring, supervising, and terminating employees and independent contractors; (c) acquiring and disposing of inventory, equipment, and supplies; (d) establishing and managing bank accounts and credit facilities; (e) setting prices and product mix; and (f) all such other acts as are reasonably necessary or appropriate to operate the business of the Company in the ordinary course.

3.3 **Member Approval Required.** Notwithstanding Section 3.2, the following actions require the affirmative approval of Members holding at least a majority of the Percentage Interests, given in writing or at a meeting of Members: (a) any amendment to this Agreement or to the Articles of Organization (which, per Section 8.3, requires unanimous Member consent); (b) the merger, consolidation, conversion, or sale of substantially all of the Company's assets; (c) the dissolution of the Company; (d) the admission of a new Member; (e) the issuance of any additional membership interests; (f) the incurrence of indebtedness for borrowed money in excess of fifty thousand dollars (US$50,000) in any single transaction or one hundred thousand dollars (US$100,000) in the aggregate during any fiscal year; (g) any election to change the Company's federal income tax classification under Section 1.7; (h) the entry into any related-party transaction with a Member or an affiliate of a Member, other than the Member Loans contemplated by Section 2.4; and (i) any commitment to lease real property for a term exceeding three (3) years or with aggregate rental payments exceeding one hundred fifty thousand dollars (US$150,000).

3.4 **Working Capital Reserve Notice.** Not later than seven (7) days before each quarterly distribution under Article IV, the Manager shall provide the Members with written notice of (a) the Company's net profits for the quarter, (b) the working capital reserve the Manager has established for the following quarter, and (c) the proposed distribution to each Member. If a Member raises a written objection to the reserve calculation within five (5) days of receipt of such notice, the Manager shall convene a meeting of the Members to resolve the objection before any distribution is made.

3.5 **Manager Compensation.** The Manager shall not be entitled to compensation for services rendered as Manager except as the Members may approve under Section 3.3. The Manager shall, however, be reimbursed for reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred on behalf of the Company in the ordinary course of business.

## Article IV — Allocations and Distributions

4.1 **Allocations of Profits and Losses.** Subject to Section 2.6, profits and losses of the Company for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.

4.2 **Distributions of Cash.** The Company shall make distributions of available cash to the Members on a quarterly basis, in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests, after the Manager has (a) set aside a working capital reserve sufficient, in the Manager's reasonable judgment, to cover the Company's anticipated operating expenses and obligations for the immediately following quarter, and (b) satisfied any then-outstanding Member Loan obligations under Section 2.4. Distributions that would draw the working capital reserve below an amount equal to sixty (60) days of the Company's then-trailing operating expenses require the unanimous written consent of the Members.

4.3 **Tax Distributions.** Not later than the tenth (10th) day of April of each year, the Company shall, to the extent of available cash and consistent with Section 4.2, distribute to each Member an amount sufficient to satisfy such Member's estimated federal and North Carolina income tax liability attributable to the Member's distributive share of the Company's taxable income for the immediately preceding fiscal year, calculated using the highest applicable marginal individual rate. Tax distributions shall be treated as advances against, and shall reduce dollar-for-dollar, the next subsequent distribution to such Member under Section 4.2.

4.4 **Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall end on **December 31** of each year.

## Article V — Transfers, Admission, and Withdrawal

5.1 **General Restriction on Transfer.** Except as expressly permitted by this Article V, no Member may sell, assign, pledge, mortgage, or otherwise transfer, voluntarily or involuntarily, any portion of such Member's membership interest in the Company.

5.2 **Right of First Refusal.** If a Member (the "**Selling Member**") receives a bona fide written offer from a third party to purchase all or any portion of the Selling Member's membership interest, the Selling Member shall give written notice of the offer to the Company and to the other Member (the "**Non-Selling Member**"), specifying the offered price, the proposed purchaser, and all other material terms. The Company shall have thirty (30) days from receipt of such notice to elect, by written notice to the Selling Member, to purchase the offered interest on the same terms. If the Company does not so elect, the Non-Selling Member shall have an additional thirty (30) days to elect to purchase the offered interest on the same terms. If neither the Company nor the Non-Selling Member elects to purchase within the foregoing periods, the Selling Member may consummate the proposed sale to the third party within ninety (90) days thereafter on the original terms; any sale on materially different terms, or any sale not consummated within ninety (90) days, shall again be subject to this Section 5.2.

5.3 **Permitted Transfers.** Notwithstanding Section 5.2, a Member may transfer all or any portion of such Member's membership interest without consent and without first complying with Section 5.2 to (a) a member of such Member's immediate family (defined as a spouse, child, parent, or sibling), (b) a trust the beneficiaries of which are exclusively such Member and members of such Member's immediate family, or (c) an entity all of the equity of which is owned by such Member, in each case provided that the transferee executes a written joinder to this Agreement.

5.4 **Buy-Out Upon Death or Permanent Disability.** Upon the death of a Member, or upon a determination of permanent disability of a Member, the Company shall have the right (but not the obligation), exercisable by written notice to the deceased Member's estate or to the disabled Member, as the case may be, within one hundred eighty (180) days after the event, to purchase the affected Member's entire membership interest. The purchase price shall be the average of (i) the fair-market valuation of the affected interest as determined by a qualified appraiser engaged by the Company at the Company's expense, and (ii) if the affected Member or such Member's estate so elects within thirty (30) days of receiving the Company's appraisal, a second fair-market valuation by a qualified appraiser engaged by the affected Member or estate at the affected Member's or estate's expense. For purposes of this Section 5.4, "**permanent disability**" means a Member's total and continuous inability, by reason of physical or mental illness or injury, to perform such Member's substantive duties to the Company for a period of one hundred eighty (180) consecutive days, as certified in writing by two licensed physicians, one chosen by the affected Member (or the affected Member's legal representative) and one chosen by the other Member.

5.5 **Admission of New Members.** A new Member may be admitted to the Company only upon the unanimous written consent of the existing Members. Any new Member's economic and governance rights shall be as the Members may determine and shall be set forth in an amendment to this Agreement executed by all Members.

5.6 **No Withdrawal.** No Member may voluntarily withdraw from the Company except by transferring all of such Member's membership interest in accordance with this Article V or by the dissolution of the Company.

## Article VI — Dissolution and Winding Up

6.1 **Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events: (a) the unanimous written consent of the Members; (b) the entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act; (c) the sale of substantially all of the Company's assets; or (d) any other event causing dissolution under the Act.

6.2 **Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Manager (or such other Person as the Members may designate) shall wind up the affairs of the Company. The assets of the Company shall be applied in the following order: (i) to the payment of the Company's debts and liabilities to creditors other than the Members; (ii) to the repayment of any outstanding Member Loans, in proportion to the principal and accrued interest owed; (iii) to the establishment of reserves reasonably deemed necessary by the winding-up Person for contingent liabilities; and (iv) to the Members in proportion to their respective positive capital account balances.

## Article VII — Intellectual Property

7.1 **Assignment of Pre-Formation Coffee Roasting IP.** Concurrently with the execution of this Agreement, Marcus Holloway shall execute an assignment to the Company, substantially in the form attached as **Exhibit B**, of all right, title, and interest in and to the coffee roasting formulations, blend recipes, and single-origin roast profiles developed by Marcus Holloway prior to the Effective Date and identified on **Exhibit B-1**. From and after the Effective Date, all coffee roasting formulations, blend recipes, single-origin roast profiles, marketing copy, customer lists, trade dress, and other intellectual property created by any Member or employee of the Company in the course of the Company's business shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

7.2 **Trademark.** The Members acknowledge that the Company has not yet filed for federal or state trademark protection for the name "Pine Hollow Roasters" or any associated logo or trade dress. The Members shall use commercially reasonable efforts to evaluate and pursue trademark protection within twelve (12) months of the Effective Date.

## Article VIII — Restrictive Covenants

8.1 **Non-Competition.** Each Member agrees that, during the period beginning on the date such Member's membership interest is transferred or otherwise terminated by reason of the Member's voluntary withdrawal or a buy-out under Section 5.4, and ending two (2) years thereafter, such Member shall not, directly or indirectly, own, operate, manage, or have a financial interest of greater than five percent (5%) in any business engaged in coffee roasting or in the wholesale distribution of roasted coffee, in any case within a twenty-five (25) mile radius of the Company's principal office. This Section 8.1 shall not restrict a former Member from being employed by an unaffiliated retail establishment (such as a café, restaurant, or grocery store) that sells coffee products supplied by a third party.

8.2 **Customer Non-Solicitation.** Each Member agrees that, for a period of twelve (12) months after such Member's membership interest is transferred or otherwise terminated, such Member shall not solicit, for the purpose of providing coffee roasting or wholesale coffee distribution services, any Person that was a wholesale customer of the Company during the twelve (12) months immediately preceding such termination.

8.3 **Reasonableness.** Each Member acknowledges that the restrictions in Sections 8.1 and 8.2 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geography and are necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of the Company. If a court of competent jurisdiction determines that any restriction in this Article VIII is unenforceable as written, the court shall be authorized to modify the restriction to the minimum extent necessary to render it enforceable, and the restriction as so modified shall be enforced.

## Article IX — Dispute Resolution

9.1 **Negotiation.** In the event of any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to this Agreement or the Company (a "**Dispute**"), the Members shall first attempt to resolve the Dispute through good-faith negotiation for a period of not less than thirty (30) days after written notice of the Dispute has been delivered by one Member to the other.

9.2 **Mediation.** If the Dispute is not resolved through negotiation under Section 9.1, the Members shall submit the Dispute to non-binding mediation before a mediator selected by mutual agreement of the Members from the Buncombe County Mediation Center roster or, if the Members cannot agree on a mediator within fifteen (15) days, a mediator appointed by the American Arbitration Association. Mediation shall be commenced within sixty (60) days after the conclusion of the negotiation period and shall be conducted in Asheville, North Carolina.

9.3 **Binding Arbitration.** If the Dispute is not resolved through mediation under Section 9.2 within ninety (90) days after the mediation is commenced, the Dispute shall be finally resolved by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator in Asheville, North Carolina. Judgment on the arbitrator's award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

9.4 **Injunctive Relief.** Notwithstanding Sections 9.1 through 9.3, any Member or the Company may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction at any time to prevent or restrain conduct that, if not enjoined, would cause irreparable harm to the Company (including, without limitation, conduct violating Articles VII or VIII).

## Article X — Indemnification

10.1 **Indemnification of Members and Manager.** The Company shall indemnify each Member and the Manager, to the fullest extent permitted by the Act, against any and all liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) incurred in connection with the Company's business, except for acts or omissions constituting gross negligence, willful misconduct, fraud, or a knowing violation of law.

## Article XI — Miscellaneous

11.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of North Carolina, without regard to its conflicts-of-law principles.

11.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Articles of Organization, the exhibits and schedules attached hereto, and the assignment of intellectual property contemplated by Section 7.1, constitutes the entire agreement among the Members with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous oral or written agreements.

11.3 **Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by all Members.

11.4 **Spousal Consent.** Each Member who is or becomes married shall use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain a written spousal consent in substantially the form attached as **Exhibit C** acknowledging that the Member's spouse will not assert any independent right to vote, transfer, or encumber the Member's interest in the Company. The spousal consent does not waive or modify any equitable distribution or other marital-property right of the spouse under applicable law; it operates solely with respect to the governance of the membership interest within the Company.

11.5 **Notices.** All notices, requests, and other communications under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given upon (a) personal delivery, (b) the third business day after deposit in the United States mail, registered or certified, return receipt requested, postage prepaid, or (c) confirmed delivery by an internationally recognized overnight courier, in each case addressed to the recipient at the address set forth on Schedule A or such other address as the recipient may designate by notice given in accordance with this Section.

11.6 **Counterparts; Electronic Signatures.** This Agreement may be executed in one or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures and electronically delivered signature pages shall have the same legal force and effect as original signatures and originally delivered signature pages.

11.7 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the validity, legality, and enforceability of the remaining provisions shall not in any way be affected or impaired.

11.8 **Headings.** The headings used in this Agreement are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of any provision.

## Ambiguities flagged by drafting agent

1. **Roaster appraisal exhibit (Exhibit A).** The intake response and the consultation confirm that Marcus has a written appraisal of the Probat UG-22 dated March 14, 2026, valuing it at US$42,000. The appraisal itself has not yet been provided to the firm. Need to attach before signing.
2. **Recipe inventory (Exhibit B-1).** Marcus committed during the consultation to deliver a written inventory of the four house blends and seven single-origin profiles within two weeks. The OA references Exhibit B-1; the inventory needs to be received and attached before signing.
3. **Life insurance funding.** Section 5.4 establishes the buy-out mechanism for death and disability but does not require funding through life insurance. Juan Carlos and the Members agreed during consultation that the Members will revisit this question with their insurance broker within six months of formation. Not a drafting gap, but worth flagging in the cover memo so it doesn't get forgotten.
4. **Spousal consent for Sarah Holloway.** Section 11.4 references a spousal-consent exhibit (Exhibit C). Marcus is married; Sarah needs to sign this. Priya is not married; no consent needed for her.
5. **Trademark filing.** Section 7.2 commits the Members to evaluate trademark protection within twelve months. The intake response excludes trademark filing from this engagement. Confirm in the engagement letter that trademark work is a separate matter so there is no confusion about scope.
6. **Bank account and EIN.** Not addressed in the OA, which is consistent with practice (these are operational matters not requiring OA treatment), but the Manager should be prompted on the cover memo to apply for the Company's EIN and open the Company bank account within the first thirty days post-formation.
