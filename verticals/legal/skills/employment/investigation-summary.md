## Before you begin — privilege notice

**Marking does not create privilege.** Every output from this skill should carry a confidentiality label (see headers below), but the label does not itself establish privilege. Whether the underlying investigation is actually privileged depends on whether it was attorney-directed and for the purpose of obtaining legal advice.

**Confirm before drafting:** Is this investigation attorney-directed? If HR is running it with legal in an advisory role, or if it was not initiated at the direction of counsel for the purpose of obtaining legal advice, the privilege analysis changes materially and the default labels below may be misleading. If there is any doubt, surface the question to the attorney before creating any summary.

**Distribution discipline.** Every summary this skill produces inherits the privilege and confidentiality status of the underlying investigation. Forwarding to non-attorneys outside the investigation team, cc'ing HR without scoping, or distributing to the business side without authorization can waive privilege over the entire investigation. Every distribution decision should be made deliberately.

---

## Identify the matter and source material

If a matter is in context, ground the summary in it. If no matter is active, ask the attorney: "Which matter is this for, and can you share the investigation memo or notes you want me to summarize?"

If no investigation memo or notes exist yet, offer to help structure the investigation first before drafting a summary.

---

## Identify the audience and purpose

Ask — if not already clear:

1. **Who is the audience?** HR / leadership or board / outside counsel
2. **What decision or action does this summary support?** (e.g., disciplinary action, governance decision, litigation handoff)

The audience determines which content is included and which must be stripped. These are not stylistic choices — they are privilege and legal exposure decisions.

---

## Audience-specific summary rules

### HR summary (for HR decision on disciplinary action)

**Include:**
- What happened — factual narrative only, no legal analysis
- Finding on each allegation: Sustained / Not Sustained / Inconclusive
- Recommended action (if the attorney has reached one)

**Do NOT include — strip entirely:**
- Attorney mental impressions
- Credibility methodology or reasoning
- Legal exposure analysis or litigation risk
- Entry IDs, document citations, or references to the investigation log
- Any content that would reveal the attorney's analytical process

**Header to apply:**
> CONFIDENTIAL — HR USE ONLY — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE

**After drafting, remind the attorney:** This version is intentionally stripped of privilege-sensitive content. Confirm it does not inadvertently include anything from the above exclusion list before distributing to HR.

---

### Leadership or Board summary (for governance decision)

**Include:**
- The allegation and scope in one paragraph
- Key findings (high level)
- Business impact or exposure — high level only, no specific legal analysis
- What the company is or will be doing about it

**Do NOT include:**
- Detailed credibility assessments
- Specific legal exposure analysis
- Attorney mental impressions or analytical methodology

**Header to apply:**
> CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE WITHOUT COUNSEL APPROVAL

---

### Outside Counsel briefing (for litigation handoff or deeper legal review)

**Include:**
- Full context including legal exposure analysis
- Findings on each allegation with the evidence basis
- Open evidentiary threads not fully resolved
- Credibility issues that remain contested
- Documents that would be most significant in litigation
- Upjohn warning status

**Header to apply:**
> CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT — PREPARED IN ANTICIPATION OF LITIGATION

---

## Consequential-action gate — external responses

**If the attorney indicates this summary will be used to respond to an external demand, charge, or complaint** (EEOC charge response, plaintiff's-counsel demand letter, regulator inquiry, or any formal complaint reply), stop before drafting and flag:

> Responding to a demand, charge, or complaint has legal consequences — positions taken here are admissions in later proceedings, privilege over the investigation can be inadvertently waived, and affirmative defenses can be forfeited. Please confirm you want to proceed and that the response has been reviewed (or will be reviewed) before it is sent.

If the attorney confirms, proceed. If uncertain, present a brief for their review covering:
- The allegation, forum, and deadline
- What the investigation surfaced (findings by allegation; interviews conducted; Upjohn warnings given or not)
- Any unresolved evidentiary threads or credibility contests
- What the proposed response says and what it implicitly concedes
- Risk of privilege waiver, inconsistent factual statements, or missed affirmative defenses

Internal memos, HR summaries, and leadership briefings used only within the organization do not trigger this gate — but the privilege caveat above still applies to all outputs.

## Summary output structure

Present the draft in chat for the attorney to review. Do not treat this as a final document — the attorney should review, edit, and save it to the matter.

**HR summary format:**

```
CONFIDENTIAL — HR USE ONLY — DO NOT DISTRIBUTE

Internal Investigation Summary
Matter: [Matter name]
Date: [Date]
Prepared by: [Attorney name — attorney to fill in]

Allegation
[Plain-language description of what was investigated]

Investigation Scope
[Interviews conducted (names/roles); document categories reviewed; period covered]

Findings
| Allegation | Finding |
|---|---|
| [Allegation 1] | Sustained / Not Sustained / Inconclusive |
| [Allegation 2] | ... |

Recommended Action
[To be filled in by attorney]
```

**Leadership/Board summary format:**

```
CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT
DO NOT DISTRIBUTE WITHOUT COUNSEL APPROVAL

Investigation Briefing — [Matter name]
Date: [Date]
Prepared by: [Attorney name — attorney to fill in]

Background
[The allegation and scope in one paragraph]

Key Findings
[Bullet points — what the investigation concluded on each allegation]

Business Impact
[High-level — no specific legal analysis]

Next Steps
[What the company is doing or will do]
```

**Outside Counsel briefing format:**

```
CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT
PREPARED IN ANTICIPATION OF LITIGATION
DO NOT DISTRIBUTE

Investigation Briefing for Outside Counsel — [Matter name]
Date: [Date]
Prepared by: [Attorney name — attorney to fill in]

Matter Overview
[Allegation, investigation type, period]

Findings by Allegation
[Each allegation with finding and evidence basis]

Legal Exposure Assessment
[To be filled in / reviewed by attorney]

Open Evidentiary Threads
[Anything not fully resolved]

Contested Credibility
[Any witnesses where credibility is disputed and unresolved]

Key Documents for Litigation
[Documents of greatest significance]

Upjohn Warning Status
[Confirmed / Not confirmed / Partial — note which interviews]
```

## What this skill does NOT do

- Make disciplinary decisions — that is the attorney's and HR's determination
- Guarantee privilege — privilege depends on how the investigation was structured, not how documents are labeled
- Replace the full investigation memo — this is a stripped summary; the memo and log remain the authoritative record
- Process documents directly — if documents need to be reviewed for summary purposes, the attorney should provide the relevant excerpts or notes

---

## After producing the summary

Ask the attorney:

1. Does this summary reflect your findings accurately?
2. Is there anything in the draft that should not be distributed to this audience?
3. Do you want to adjust the recommended action section before sharing?

Present the next steps as options:
- Revise the summary based on attorney feedback
- Draft a summary for a different audience
- Help prepare the full investigation memo if one has not been drafted
- Draft a response to an external demand (requires confirmation of review before finalizing)
- Something else

**The attorney owns the findings, the legal conclusions, and the decision to distribute. Every output here is a draft for attorney review — not a final document, not legal advice, and not a substitute for the attorney's judgment.**

> Jurisdiction note: This skill applies US employment law frameworks by default. When union representation is in the picture, Weingarten rights may apply to investigatory interviews. When the employer is a public entity, Garrity protections may apply to compelled employee statements. If either flag is relevant, surface it to the attorney before the summary is finalized. North Carolina follows federal frameworks on these issues with limited state-specific additions; surface any NC-specific questions to the attorney.
